What is VoIP?

The modern alternative to landlines

VoIP stands for Voice over Internet Protocol and acts as technology that allows you to make phone calls using the internet. The biggest difference between VoIP and traditional phones is the need for physical wiring. VoIP is not bound to a single location because of this.

With a trusted VoIP service provider to handle the calls and some high speed internet, you'll be up and running in no time. VoIP is cheaper, more efficient, and significantly quicker to setup than traditional landlines. Some of the main benefits are listed below:

Lower costs of operations

Increased accessibility

Full portability

Greater scalability

Higher voice quality

Call recording

Automated services and assistance

VoIP Features

VoIP is a powerful business tool that offers tons of features to help you operate. Some of these features include:

Advanced call management

With advanced call management, you can prioritize calls by holding, routing or blocking incoming calls.

Call routing

Call routing helps your business organize and direct calls depending on certain variables such as: customer type, issue, agent type, etc.

Screen sharing

With remote work being much more common, screen sharing can help with coordination of work and productivity.

Call recording

VoIP makes it easier to record calls that can be used later to help with recalling key points of the conversation.
